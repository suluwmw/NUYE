Shiver Randall D. Standridge

Shiver is a great autumnal song that gives good imagery of a cold night. The students learn how to tremolo in this piece and play back and forth with the melodies.

Nightmare On the Housetop Traditional arr. John M. Pasternak

Nightmare On the Housetop is a take on the traditional piece Up On The Housetop. The composer says that children are excited for Santa to come but then they are surprised with Halloween characters instead. This piece goes back and forth with bow (arco) and plucking (pizzicato).

Into the Midnight Forest Mekel Rogers

Into the Midnight Forest has a "mysterious march through the darkness" feel that the composer wants. It's a fun piece that bring in great dynamics and a lot of staccato (short) bowings. It also brings in a few legato (smooth) bowings as well to contrast.

Dance of the Tumblers from Snow Maiden Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov arr. by Sandra Dackow

This piece, Dance of the Tumblers, accompanies the energetic dancing of Russian travelling entertainers as the villagers wait for a contest to see who will win Snow Maiden's heart.

Midnight Storm Erica Donahoe

Dramatic eighth-note phrases and multiple volume shifts evoke the experience of a storm blowing in after dark. The piece begins with a vigorous driving rhythm, broken by pauses between gusts of wind. Thunder growls ominously in the distance before the violins lead with a soaring melody in the middle section, perhaps mimicking the brief calm before the real energy of the storm arrives.

Shackelford Banks Jay Bocook

Named for the uninhabited island off the North Carolina coast where wild horses still roam, this energetic original features a distinctive western flavor.

March of the Shadows Haley Woodrow

Paying homage to the traditional march form, this work steps outside the box in this delightfully dark and unique piece in minor form.

Pure Imagination Leslie Bricusse & Anthony Newley, arr. Paul Murtha

Originally sung by Gene Wilder in the 1971 movie classic "Willy Wonka the Chocolate Factory," this timeless song has remained a favorite ever since. This setting is based on the version sung by Timothée Chalamet for the movie "Wonka" and features lush harmonies and an appealing pop groove. Magical!

Egmont Overture

Ludwig van Beethoven
(BAY-toh-ven)
The Egmont Overture has been called the "distilled essence of Beethoven." Written during his middle period, it still relies on classical structure but adds the narrative arc and powerful emotions of Romanticism.

Violin Concerto No. 1 in A minor

Jean-Baptiste Accolay
(AH-coh-lay)
The liner notes from famous violinist Itzhak Perlman's album Concertos from my Childhood refers to Accolay as 'long-forgotten'. But this piece hasn't been. It's a one-movement gem of lyric romanticism, frequently used to showcase multiple bowing techniques and left-hand dexterity.

"Hungarian March" from The Damnation of Faust

Hector Berlioz
(BEHR-lee-ohz)
The dramatic and exuberent "Hungarian March" occurs Act I of Berlioz's The Damnation of Faust. It's full of vitality and energy, in contrast to the title character.

Carmen Suite No. 1

Georges Bizet
(bee-ZAY)
Carmen was a shock to French opera-goers, and that was after it was toned down. They were perfectly used to seeing melodrama but they weren't used to seeing the actual death acted onstage. And the heroine is not at all conventional - instead she's from the working classes - and a flamboyant gypsy who does as she pleases.
