Hungarian March from The Damnation of Faust

Hector Berlioz
(bear-lee-OHZ)
Berlioz’s “Hungarian March” from The Damnation of Faust showcases his dramatic orchestration and colorful use of the orchestra. Written in the early 1840s, this march illustrates his fascination with Hungarian musical styles and rhythmic vitality. The work features energetic rhythms, bold brass writing, and lively string passages.
